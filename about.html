Our Story

A new category of service
for a long-neglected problem

"The homeowner with a $900k house in West Seattle has a wealth manager for their portfolio and a tax attorney for their returns. They have nobody doing that job for their largest physical asset."

That's the gap West Seattle Home Asset Management was built to fill. We exist because every service in the home industry owns a piece of the problem — but nobody owns the whole thing.

Contractors execute work within their trade silo. Home inspectors file reports, then leave. Handymen fix what's in front of them. Financial planners acknowledge the equity in your home but offer no strategies to protect or grow it. Real estate agents protect your transaction.

Nobody manages the asset itself.

We're building a new category — Home Asset Management — that combines the diagnostic rigor of a professional inspection, the coordination capability of a general contractor, and the long-term planning orientation of a financial advisor, all applied to the single largest asset most families own.

How We Operate

Principles, not just services

Fiduciary Orientation

We act in your home's best interest, not in the interest of generating repair revenue. Our inspection report is designed to stand alone as a professional deliverable — valuable regardless of whether any subsequent work is needed.

Proactive, Not Reactive

The industry defaults to reactive maintenance. We default to planned intervention. A $1,000 annual inspection is the kind of investment that prevents a $40,000 emergency — and that math is legible to anyone who's owned a home long enough.

Accountability Over Convenience

We are the single operator accountable for your home's condition. Not a marketplace. Not a directory. When something is missed or mismanaged, there's one phone number to call — ours.

West Seattle First

We are a West Seattle business, built for West Seattle homeowners. We know the housing stock, the weather patterns, the neighborhoods. Geographic focus isn't a limitation — it's a quality commitment.
